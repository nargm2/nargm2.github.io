Joy in Pixels

Reflection on My Creative Growth

As I compiled this collection of my previous works, I found myself reminiscing about the past. Long ago my only desire was to take beautiful, captivating photographs. I wanted to capture the intricacies of human experience through my work and present it to the world. Then, one day, that dream transformed.

It wasn’t a sudden transformation, make no mistake. But somewhere along the way I discovered a love for design. Maybe it stems from the young girl inside me who loved a good color palette. Or perhaps the editor-in-chief desired a new media to express their worldview through. Regardless, something shifted. I spent less time behind the camera and more time in the design studio.

My acquisition of the media design assistant position only fueled my interest. I found any and every reason to utilize my design skills in Canva. Need a photo with text over top to display on an assignment? I’ve got you! Want to personalize a video assignment with a cool slideshow? Look no further! Submitting a research paper and need a presentation to explain your findings? An hour and some elbow grease is all I need!

Now I am nearing the end of my college years and the beginning of my full-time professional life. I’m scrolling through each project, each assignment, each passion, and realizing that my designs are an expression of my desires. I desire to live creatively and freely, with choices to make at every turn. Each design is a mini life. It begins as a blank page and an idea, then it gets a name. Suddenly there is color and shape, and now words appear. An image is incorporated. A graphic included. Sometimes things come in and eventually leave, but all of it is a process. All of it is free. All of it is creative. That is why I love design. Freedom to create and inspire.

Nevaeh Randles
